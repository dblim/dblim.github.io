Geometric Invariant Theory

Moduli functors, GIT and semistability of the gradient morphism for ternary forms
My undergraduate thesis. Most of the material is expository, except the last chapter where I extend a result of Alper and Isaev to the case of ternary cubics. What I prove is the following. Consider the rational map $$\nabla : \operatorname{Sym}^3 \mathbf{C}^3 \dashrightarrow \operatorname{Gr}(3, \operatorname{Sym}^2 \mathbf{C}^3)$$ sending a ternary cubic $f$ to its span of partial derivatives $\langle f_x, f_y, f_x \rangle$. This restricts to a well-defined morphism on the GIT-semistable locus of the domain $(\operatorname{Sym}^3 \mathbf{C}^3)^{\text{ss}}$. I prove that the image of a GIT-semistable form is a GIT-semistable subspace in the Grassmannian.

A year or so after submitting my undergraduate thesis, Fedorchuk generalized this to the case of arbitrary forms of degree $d$ in $n$-variables. See Theorem 1.4.1 here, and the paragraph below the theorem acknowledging my work.
